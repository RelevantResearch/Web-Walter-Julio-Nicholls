Cities and Social Movements: Immigrant Rights Activism in the US, France, and the Netherlands, 1970-2015

By Walter J. Nicholls and Justus Uitermark. This book examines how small resistances against restrictive immigration policies develop into large and sustained mobilizations in three countries over five decades.

The book presents a bottom-up theory of movements, exploring how geography affects the evolution of social movements. It provides insights for students, scholars, policy makers, and activists. Get it today
